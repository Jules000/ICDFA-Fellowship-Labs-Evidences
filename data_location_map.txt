Persistent vs. temporary locations
-----------------------------------
- Persistent: /home and /etc keep their contents across reboots (e.g. /opt shows a
  modification date of May 10 2025, over a year old — nothing has touched it since,
  proving it survives reboots untouched).
- Temporary/runtime: /tmp and /run both showed very recent modification timestamps
  (21:09 and 21:14 today) despite being freshly created system directories — their
  contents are expected to be cleared or recreated at each boot.

Special virtual filesystems
-----------------------------------
/proc and /sys reported a Size of 0 in `ls -ld`, unlike every other directory listed.
This confirms they are not backed by real disk storage — they are generated live by
the kernel to expose running process and hardware/kernel state, which is why
/proc/uptime returns different values every time it is read.

Safety note
-----------------------------------
All commands used above are read-only (ls, find, stat, cat). No files were created,
modified, or deleted outside the intended /tmp test/verification steps.
